# Pricing

Pricing should support the first revenue product while leaving room for the larger AI Business OS platform.

## Pricing Philosophy

Customers should pay for business outcomes, not raw AI usage.

The offer should be understandable as an AI marketing department, not a bundle of prompts or tools.

## First Product Offer

The first commercial offer should be AI Marketing Team.

Potential packaging dimensions:

- Number of AI employees.
- Included workflows.
- Monthly content volume.
- Landing page support.
- CRM Lite usage.
- Analytics depth.
- Support level.
- Setup and onboarding service.

## Revenue Model Options

Possible revenue models:

- Setup fee plus monthly subscription.
- Monthly subscription with tiered packages.
- Managed service package for early customers.
- Add-ons for extra employees, channels, or support.

## Pricing Principles

- Price around value and saved execution time.
- Keep early offers simple.
- Avoid exposing token or model costs as the main customer concept.
- Protect margins by tracking provider costs.
- Use onboarding and support effort to validate pricing.

## Metrics To Watch

- Customer acquisition cost.
- Setup effort per customer.
- Gross margin.
- Time to first approved output.
- Monthly active usage.
- Support load.
- Churn risk.
