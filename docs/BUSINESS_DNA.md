# Business DNA

Business DNA is the structured identity of a customer business.

It gives AI employees the context needed to produce useful, consistent, brand-safe work.

## Purpose

Business DNA should help the system understand:

- What the business sells.
- Who it serves.
- Why customers buy.
- How the brand speaks.
- What offers and services exist.
- What constraints and promises matter.
- Which languages, regions, and channels are relevant.

## Core Fields

The first Business DNA version should include:

- Business name
- Industry
- Services and products
- Ideal customers
- Pain points
- Differentiators
- Brand voice
- Languages
- Locations served
- Offers
- Competitors
- Restrictions
- Marketing goals
- Contact and conversion details

## Ownership

Client Template owns each customer's Business DNA.

Master Admin may define the schema, templates, defaults, and update strategy.

## Versioning

Business DNA should be versioned because changes affect AI output quality and auditability.

Important changes should record who changed the profile, when it changed, and why.

## AI Usage

Business DNA should feed prompt architecture, knowledge retrieval, employee behavior, content review, and analytics interpretation.
