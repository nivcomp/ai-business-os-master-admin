# Media By Media Rollout

AI Marketing Team should not launch as a general employee team that claims to know every channel at once.

The product should prove one media type at a time, then combine proven capabilities into stronger employees and packages.

## Rollout Principle

Build the team by validated media capability:

1. Text and copy.
2. Image.
3. Landing page.
4. Video.
5. Publishing.
6. Analytics.
7. Automation.

Each stage should produce a real workflow before the next stage becomes a sales promise.

## Stage 1: Text And Copy

Primary employee: Copywriter.

Goal: prove the system can use Business DNA to create useful marketing copy.

Capabilities:

- Social post draft.
- Email draft.
- Ad copy variations.
- Landing page headline and section copy.
- CTA improvements.

Validation target:

- Level 5 for social post draft before selling copy as a package capability.

Why first:

- Lowest provider complexity.
- Fastest demo value.
- Directly proves Business DNA and approval workflow.

## Stage 2: Image

Primary employee: Designer.

Goal: prove the system can turn marketing briefs into usable visual concepts.

Capabilities:

- Image prompt creation.
- Social image concept.
- Ad creative variation.
- Thumbnail draft.
- Brand-consistent visual direction.

Validation target:

- Level 3 demo before showing image generation in sales demos.
- Level 5 before selling Designer as an add-on employee.

## Stage 3: Landing Page

Primary employees: Marketing Manager, Copywriter, Designer.

Goal: create a simple landing page draft from Business DNA and campaign goal.

Capabilities:

- Offer structure.
- Page outline.
- Hero copy.
- Sections and CTAs.
- Visual brief.
- Approval-ready landing page draft.

Validation target:

- Level 3 demo with sample business.
- Level 4 before pilot customer use.

## Stage 4: Video

Primary employee: Video Creator.

Goal: move from script and storyboard into generated or provider-assisted video assets.

Capabilities:

- Short video script.
- Storyboard.
- Shot list.
- Voiceover script.
- Generated clip draft.

Validation target:

- Start with script and storyboard as Level 5.
- Keep generated video at Level 2 or Level 3 until provider reliability is proven.

## Stage 5: Publishing

Primary employee: Campaign Manager.

Goal: prepare and schedule approved content without losing human control.

Capabilities:

- Channel adaptation.
- Schedule suggestion.
- Publish-ready post.
- Approved scheduling.
- Publish status tracking.

Validation target:

- Level 3 with mock publishing.
- Level 4 with one real channel.
- Level 5 only after permissions, audit logs, and error handling work.

## Stage 6: Analytics

Primary employee: Analytics Manager.

Goal: explain what happened and recommend what to do next.

Capabilities:

- Summarize approved and published work.
- Summarize basic engagement.
- Identify best-performing content.
- Recommend next experiment.

Validation target:

- Level 3 with demo metrics.
- Level 4 with one real analytics source.

## Stage 7: Automation

Primary employee: Marketing Manager with Campaign Manager.

Goal: automate repeatable marketing cycles after the individual media workflows are proven.

Capabilities:

- Weekly content plan.
- Draft generation queue.
- Approval reminders.
- Scheduled publishing after approval.
- Performance-based next actions.

Validation target:

- Level 2 for internal demo.
- Level 3 for guided automation.
- Level 5 only for clearly bounded automations.

## First Sellable Sequence

The safest first sales package is:

1. Business DNA onboarding.
2. Copywriter social post workflow.
3. Approval Center.
4. Basic content storage.
5. Basic client health in Master Admin.

Then add:

1. Designer image concepts.
2. Landing page draft.
3. Video script and storyboard.
4. Publishing preparation.
5. Analytics summary.
6. Guided automation.
