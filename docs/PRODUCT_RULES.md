# Product Rules

## PR-001: Revenue moves the project forward

Every milestone must move us closer to paying customers.

## PR-002: Build the smallest product people will pay for

Do not build the whole vision first. Build the smallest valuable product that can generate revenue and prove demand.

## PR-003: Sell outcomes, not tools

The customer is buying results, execution and business progress — not AI, prompts or integrations.

## PR-004: People before technology

The user experience should feel like hiring and managing an AI team, not operating a technical agent dashboard.

## PR-005: Reusable core before one-off features

Every major component should be reusable across future products.

## PR-006: Human approval protects trust

AI-generated work should be easy to review, change and approve.

## PR-007: Internal complexity, external simplicity

The internal system may be complex, but the customer experience must stay simple.

## PR-008: Do not chase every AI trend

New tools and models should be adopted only if they improve business value, cost, reliability or product quality.

## PR-009: Documentation first for major features

Major features should be preceded by a decision, PRD or architecture note.

## PR-010: Keep the client template cloneable

Any client-facing feature must work in a cloneable system and should not depend on hard-coded client-specific data.
