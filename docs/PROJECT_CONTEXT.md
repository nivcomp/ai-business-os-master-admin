# Project Context

AI Business OS is a long-term platform for building, managing, deploying and improving AI-powered business teams.

## Core Direction

We are not building only an app. We are building the foundation of a software company.

The first commercial product is AI Marketing Team: a customer-facing AI team that helps business owners create content, plan campaigns, build landing pages, approve work, publish content, analyze results and improve conversion.

## Two Repository Architecture

- `ai-business-os-master-admin`: the private control center used by Yaniv to manage clients, templates, prompts, employees, versions, updates and support.
- `ai-business-os-client-template`: the cloneable client system used as a base for each client installation.

## Why This Exists

Small and medium businesses need business outcomes, not disconnected AI tools. They need marketing execution, faster campaign cycles, consistent brand voice, clear approvals, and measurable improvement.

AI Business OS turns AI capabilities into business roles, workflows, departments, and managed outputs.

## Customer Language

Customers should experience the product as a business team:

- AI Business Team
- Employees
- Departments
- Office
- Reception
- Training
- Performance Review
- Approval Center

## Internal Technical Language

Internally, the system may use:

- Agents
- Engines
- Modules
- Provider Layers
- Prompt Blocks
- Packages
- Registries
- Update Packages

## Current Strategy

Revenue first, platform always.

The first product must be sellable fast, but every major component should strengthen the long-term AI Business OS platform.

## Primary Build Hypothesis

A focused AI Marketing Team can create enough customer value to sell before the full platform exists.

The first product should prove:

- Business owners understand the employee/team metaphor.
- Human approval creates trust.
- Business DNA improves output quality.
- Repeatable onboarding is possible.
- A cloneable Client Template can support multiple customers.

## Near-Term Constraints

- Avoid building the entire future OS before revenue.
- Avoid hard-coded client-specific work.
- Avoid provider lock-in where an abstraction is practical.
- Avoid technical UI language in customer-facing surfaces.
- Avoid automation that bypasses trust and approval too early.
