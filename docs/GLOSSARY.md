# Glossary

## AI Business OS
The long-term platform vision: a modular operating system for business work powered by AI employees, agents, engines and reusable modules.

## AI Marketing Team
The first commercial product. A customer-facing AI marketing department that helps businesses create, approve, publish and analyze marketing work.

## Master Admin
The private control center used by Yaniv to manage client systems, templates, updates, agents, packages, prompts and support access.

## Client Template
The cloneable client system that can be copied for every new customer. Each clone should have its own business data, API keys, settings and Business DNA.

## Business DNA
The structured identity of a business: offers, services, audience, brand voice, languages, tone, products, competitors, restrictions and marketing goals.

## Employee
The customer-facing name for an AI worker. Internally, an Employee may be powered by one or more agents, tools, prompts and workflows.

## Agent
An internal technical AI unit with instructions, role, permissions, tools, memory and measurable output.

## Department
A customer-facing group of employees, such as Marketing, Creative, Sales, Analytics or Management.

## Engine
A reusable internal capability, such as Prompt Engine, Agent Engine, Landing Page Engine, Knowledge Engine, Analytics Engine or Publishing Engine.

## Module
A feature area that can be enabled, disabled, upgraded or packaged. Examples: CRM Lite, Landing Pages, Social Publishing, Analytics, WhatsApp, Email.

## Package
A bundle of employees, prompts, modules, settings and templates that can be installed or updated.

## Provider Layer
An abstraction layer that prevents lock-in to one provider, such as one AI model, email service, WhatsApp provider, image model or analytics provider.

## Prompt Architecture
The system for composing reusable prompt blocks from employee role, Business DNA, knowledge, workflow state, output format, and safety rules.

## Knowledge Base
A customer-specific or package-specific store of useful information that AI employees can retrieve when producing work.

## Memory
Controlled persistence of useful context across interactions, scoped by customer and governed by privacy and quality rules.

## Tool
A controlled capability that an AI employee may use, such as retrieving knowledge, creating a draft, analyzing data, or preparing a message.

## Approval Center
The customer-facing place where AI-generated work is reviewed, changed, approved, rejected, published, or archived.

## Update Center
The Master Admin capability for publishing package, module, template, prompt, and employee updates to client installations.

## Client Health
A Master Admin view of whether a client is onboarded, active, up to date, using the product, and experiencing errors or support issues.

## Cloneability
The ability to create a new client system from the Client Template without hard-coded client data or manual one-off engineering.

## Tenant
A client installation or customer context with isolated data, settings, credentials, users, analytics, and package state.

## First Value
The moment a customer completes enough onboarding to approve a useful AI-generated output.
