# Customer Lifecycle

The customer lifecycle defines how a customer moves from first interest to long-term value.

## Stages

1. Lead
2. Qualified prospect
3. Demo
4. Proposal
5. Closed customer
6. Onboarding
7. First value
8. Active usage
9. Expansion
10. Renewal or churn prevention

## First Value

First value should happen when the customer completes Business DNA and approves a useful marketing output created by an AI employee.

The product should optimize for time to first approved output.

## Lifecycle Ownership

Sales owns lead to close.

Onboarding owns close to first value.

Product owns repeated value.

Support owns issue resolution and trust recovery.

Master Admin should eventually provide visibility into every lifecycle stage.

## Health Signals

Customer health signals include:

- Business DNA completed.
- First draft created.
- First approval completed.
- Repeat employee usage.
- Publishing or content export.
- Low support friction.
- Positive feedback.
